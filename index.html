COCONUT TREE

Cocos Nucifera

The Coconut Tree is a tall tropical palm called the “Tree of Life” because every part is useful. It provides coconut water, edible kernel, coir, leaves, and wood, making it valuable for food, shelter, and daily needs. It is commonly grown in coastal regions of Asia and other tropical areas.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Cocos Nucifera
  • Family: Arecaceae (Palm family)
  • Native Region: Southeast Asia
  • Height: 15–30 meters tall
  • Notable Feature:Large nut containing refreshing water and edible kernel
  • Lifespan: ~60–80 years

Leaves, Flowers, and Fruit

  • Leaves: Long, feather-like fronds (4–6 m in length).
  • Flowers: Small, yellowish-white, unisexual flowers in clusters.
  • Fruit: Large drupe (the coconut) with a hard shell, fibrous husk, and edible kernel inside.

Uses

The fruit provides water, milk, oil, and edible kernel, while the husk yields coir for ropes and mats. Its leaves are used for roofing and weaving, and the trunk gives timber for construction. Even the shell is used for fuel and handicrafts.

⚠️ Safety Note: While coconut water is safe, excessive intake may cause stomach upset in some people.
