GOLDEN SHOWER TREE

Cassia fistula

The Golden Shower Tree is a beautiful ornamental tree known for its long hanging clusters of bright yellow flowers. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia, and is often planted along roadsides and in gardens for its spectacular summer bloom.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Cassia fistula
  • Family: Fabaceae
  • Native Region: Indian subcontinent & Southeast Asia
  • Height: 10–20 m (commonly 5–10 m in cultivation)
  • Notable Feature: Hanging golden-yellow flower clusters
  • Lifespan: ~40–60 years

Leaves, Flowers, and Fruit

  • Leaves: Pinnate leaves with 8–20 green leaflets.
  • Flowers: Summer bloom with racemes of golden-yellow flowers.
  • Fruit: Long cylindrical pods with pulp and seeds.

Traditional Uses

In traditional medicine (Ayurveda), parts of the Golden Shower Tree are used for skin care and digestive remedies. Bark, leaves, and flowers are sometimes included in herbal treatments.

⚠️ Safety Note: These are traditional uses only and should not replace medical treatment. Always consult a healthcare professional.
